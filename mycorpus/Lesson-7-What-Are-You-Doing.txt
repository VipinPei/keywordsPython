Learning Strategy

Learning Strategies are the thoughts and actions that help make learning easier or more effective. The learning strategy for this lesson is Ask to Clarify.

In the video you see Anna ask Amelia: "What are you doing?" Amelia answers, "I'm reading." Then Anna asks a question to clarify: "Are you reading the news?

Asking a question to clarify is a very useful learning strategy, because it helps you get more information. You can also learn new words when someone explains more about what they said.

Write to us in the Comments section or send us an email about how you ask questions to clarify what you want to know. Teachers, see the Lesson Plan for more details on teaching this strategy.

New Words

boss C n. the person whose job is to tell other workers what to do

bother C v. ?to annoy someone?or?to cause someone to feel annoyed

busy C adj. actively doing something

excited C adj. very enthusiastic and eager about something

nervous C adj. having or showing feelings of being worried and afraid about what might happen

news C n. information that is reported in a newspaper, magazine, or a television news program

office C n. a building or room in which people work at desks doing business or professional activities

read C v. to look at and understand the meaning of letters, words, symbols, etc.

record C v. to store (something, such as sounds, music, images, etc.) on tape or on a disk so that it can be heard or seen later

show C n. a television or radio program

work C v. to do things as part of your job

write C v. to create (a book, poem, story, etc.) by writing words on paper or on a computer
